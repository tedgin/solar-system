The Solar System from Earth

This is a first-person view of the Solar System from Earth displaying the Moon, the Sun, and the other seven planets. The Earth is looking directly at the Solar System's center of mass. This direction forms the ẑ axis. The horizontal or x̂ axis is aligned so that the span of x̂ and ẑ forms the ecliptic plane. The vertical or ŷ axis is aligned so that the Earth orbits the Sun counterclockwise from the perspective of an observer positioned on the positive ŷ axis looking towards the origin.

This simulation isn't real-time. It's not even accurate. It's an N-body gravitational simulation. The Sun starts out at the origin at rest. All of the planets and the Moon start at their approximate real positions on 2023/01/01.
